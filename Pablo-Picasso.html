Pablo Ruiz Picasso

Pablo Ruiz Picasso was a Spanish painter, sculptor, printmaker, ceramicist and theatre designer who spent most of his adult life in France. Regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, he is known for co-founding the Cubist movement, the invention of constructed sculpture, the co-invention of collage, and for the wide variety of styles that he helped develop and explore.

25 October 1881: was born in the city of Málaga, Andalusia, in southern Spain

1891 : the family moved to A Coruna.

1895: Picasso was traumatized when his seven-year-old sister, died of diphtheria. After that, the family moved to Barcelona

1894 : his career as a painter can be said to have begun

1900 : Picasso made his first trip to Paris. There, he met the journalist and poet Max Jacob, who helped Picasso learn the language and its literature.

1901-1904: Picasso's Blue Period (characterized by sombre paintings rendered in shades of blue and blue-green only occasionally warmed by other colours)

1904-1906 : The rose period (is characterised by a lighter tone and style utilising orange and pink colours and featuring many circus people)

1907-1909 : African art and primitivism

1909-1912 : Analytic cubism, is a style of painting Picasso developed with Georges Braque using monochrome brownish and neutral colours. Both artists took apart objects and "analyzed" them in terms of their shapes.

1912-1919 : Synthetic cubism, was a further development of the genre of cubism, in which cut paper fragments, were pasted into compositions, marking the first use of collage in fine art.

1919-1929 : Neoclassicism and surrealism

1930-1939 :The Great Depression to MoMA exhibition

1944 : Picasso joined the French Communist Party.

1948 : He attended the World Congress of Intellectuals in Defense of Peace in Poland

9 January 1949, Picasso created Dove, a black and white lithograph. It was used to illustrate a poster at the 1949 World Peace Council, known as "The dove of peace". Picasso's image was used around the world as a symbol of the Peace Congresses and communism.

1950 received the Stalin Peace Prize from the Soviet government.

In 1962, he received the Lenin Peace Prize.

April 1973 : Pablo Picasso died in Mougins, France.
